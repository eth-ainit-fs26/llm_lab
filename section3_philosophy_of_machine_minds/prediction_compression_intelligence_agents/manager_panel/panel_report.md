## 3. Prioritized edit list

### P0 — must fix before using the notes

**P0-1. Replace "IQ points" in the three-currencies pitch.**
*Target:* Preface, first Intuition box opener.
*Current:* "three currencies — bits saved, dollars earned, IQ points
— and three exchange rates between them."
*Proposed:* "Think of it as three currencies — **bits saved** (how
tightly a model compresses your data), **dollars earned** (what that
compression is worth when deployed), and **capability on held-out
tasks** (how well it handles problems it has not seen). Three
currencies, three exchange rates, one underlying quantity."
*Driver:* Maria (CFO) + showman + math writer (converged).
*Why P0:* "IQ points" misleads about what the theorems quantify.
Psychometric IQ is not what Solomonoff/Hutter bound — capability on
held-out distributions is. Leaving it in conflates a theorem with a
contested construct.

**P0-2. Add an order-of-magnitude footnote to the invariance theorem.**
*Target:* `\cref{thm:invariance}`.
*Proposed footnote:* "The constant $c_{U_1,U_2}$ is finite and does
not grow with $|x|$. For pairs of realistic universal machines — two
reasonable programming languages interpreting each other — it is
typically in the hundreds to low thousands of bits. For data objects
larger than that (a megabyte-scale corpus, an ERP export), it is
numerically negligible and 'machine-independent up to an additive
constant' is a property you can plan around. For objects smaller than
the constant, it is not."
*Driver:* Maria Q2 + math writer.
*Why P0:* Without this, the invariance theorem misleads a
decision-maker about when "machine-independent" is operationally
meaningful.

**P0-3. Add a Section 8 paragraph on the only non-tautological
falsifier.**
*Target:* `\cref{sec:caveats}`.
*Proposed text:* "A reasonable operator will ask: if AIXI is
uncomputable and every deployed system is 'a finite approximation',
what test other than cross-entropy itself decides that system A
approximates the ideal better than system B? Honest answer: held-out
cross-entropy on distributions the training set did not see, plus
downstream task transfer. There is no deeper falsifier in the
current theory. If that makes the AIXI framing feel tautological to
an operator, the operator is reading the state of the field
correctly."
*Driver:* Maria Q3 + math writer (conceded as "best question in the
panel").
*Why P0:* Without this, the AIXI framing is a tautology dressed as a
north star — the exact failure mode Maria flagged and the math writer
agreed is load-bearing to fix.

**P0-4. Seed an honest-frame warning in the preface.**
*Target:* Preface, first page, after the three-currencies sentence.
*Proposed text:* "One frame to carry throughout: **a perfect
next-token predictor would be an ideal learner; real next-token
predictors are not perfect, and the gap is exactly where this
document's caveats live.** We state the thesis before we qualify it,
but the qualification is not optional."
*Driver:* Aisha (CEO) + math writer (refine, not concede).
*Why P0:* The thesis is stated strongly on page 1; the caveat is
buried in §8. Aisha would not trust the authors without a first-page
honesty flag. Maria's irritation at the slogan-version of the claim
is defused by the same sentence.

**P0-5. Cut "a theorem wearing a T-shirt."**
*Target:* §7 ("What this buys us for LLMs"), ~line 895.
*Proposed:* delete the phrase; replace with "the mathematics agrees."
*Driver:* Maria + Aisha + both authors.
*Why P0:* 2-of-3 panelists flagged it as showing off; both authors
conceded. Leaving it in signals the authors did not read their own
panel.

### P1 — strong improvements

**P1-1. Replace the town-hall-of-theorists with David's
prediction-market reframe.**
*Target:* §5 (Solomonoff), first Intuition box.
*Proposed text (showman's staging):* "Imagine a prediction market
where every trader is a theory of the world. Simple theories start
with a large float; baroque theories start with almost nothing —
their share price is their *simplicity premium.* Each new data point
is an earnings call. Theories that predicted it cheaply get paid;
theories that predicted it badly get margin-called. Over time the
market consolidates around the simplest theories that still explain
the tape. That consolidation **is** Solomonoff induction."
*Driver:* Maria (flag) + David (proposal) + showman (staging) + math
writer (accept).

**P1-2. Refine the chessplayer-with-infinite-advisors into a
regional manager.**
*Target:* §6 (AIXI), first Intuition box.
*Proposed text:* "Imagine a regional manager who, before every
staffing decision, consulted every possible theory of her stores —
every demand model, every weather-effect hypothesis, every crank
manifesto about Mercury retrograde — weighted by how simple each
theory is. She then places the staffing bet that maximises expected
sales across all of them. That manager would be provably optimal in
a precise technical sense. She would also be impossible: consulting
every possible theory takes infinite time. This is AIXI. It is a
**north star for what a learner is approximating, not a machine
anyone can build.** Real systems are food trucks reading the
Michelin menu; AIXI is the chef with an infinite pantry and no
kitchen."
*Driver:* Maria + Aisha + showman, within math writer's "uncomputable
/ Pareto-optimal" hill. Also absorbs the speed-of-light replacement.

**P1-3. Replace the Blockbuster employee with the junior analyst.**
*Target:* §8.
*Proposed text:* "A junior analyst who has memorised every deal
memo in the vault — say, **4,000 deals** going back a decade — has
compressed a lot of data. Ask her to price a deal that is not in the
vault and you find out whether she compressed the memos or the
market."
*Driver:* David (proposal) + Aisha (would not blink at it) + both
authors.

**P1-4. Rewrite the two-consultants box with the earnestness patch
and Maria's number.**
*Target:* §3, Intuition box.
*Proposed text:* "Two consultants write up the same quarter. Both
are earnest. The one who understands your business writes a
one-pager: *revenue up 4%, driven by Zurich, offset by FX.* The one
whose mental model is slightly off writes ten pages of hedging —
because every surprise in the data costs her extra sentences. Cross-
entropy is a **tax on a wrong model**, paid in pages. At 0.53 extra
bits per token over a million-token corpus that is roughly **530,000
extra bits of hedging** — a concrete, addable number."
*Driver:* David + Maria + showman.

**P1-5. Add a scaffolding intuition box defining *residual* and
*arithmetic coding* before §7's LLM-as-compressor sentence.**
*Target:* §7, preceding "weights plus arithmetic-coded residuals".
*Proposed text:* "A **residual** is the gap between what the model
predicted and what actually came next. If the model gave next-token
probability 0.9 to *'the'* and *'the'* showed up, the residual is
tiny — a fraction of a bit. If the model gave 0.01 and *'the'* still
showed up, the residual is large — about 6.6 bits of surprise.
**Arithmetic coding** is just a near-optimal way to write all those
surprise numbers down end to end. The compressed file is therefore
the model's weights (fixed cost) plus the bill for every surprise
(variable cost)."
*Driver:* Aisha + both authors.

**P1-6. Define KL divergence in the nearest Intuition box on first
prose use.** One-liner: "KL divergence measures how many extra bits
per token you pay for using the wrong distribution — the same tax as
above, formalised."
*Driver:* Aisha + math writer.

**P1-7. Promote Hutter Prize to §2 sidebar.** Pull the existing
"concrete hinge" paragraph forward from §7 to §2, unchanged.
*Driver:* David + Aisha + math writer.

**P1-8. Promote the snake-oil line to a §4 sidebar titled "The
compression sniff test."** Text: *"If a vendor claims to compress
genuinely random or encrypted data, they are selling snake oil —

*Driver:* Maria (copied it verbatim) + showman.

**P1-9. Cut "Platonic ideal" and "baroque theorist"; define or cut
"category error."** Small local edits in §4, §5, §8.
*Driver:* Aisha + both authors.

**P1-10. Cut the AIXItl / MC-AIXI name-drop; keep the sentence

*Target:* §6.
*Driver:* Aisha + math writer.
